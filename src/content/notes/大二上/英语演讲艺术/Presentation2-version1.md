Christmas is drawing near. Let me ask you a question. When you think of a Christmas hero, who do you think of? Santa Claus? Maybe a snowman? Well, if you go to Japan, the hero of Christmas is a little different. He has white hair. He has a white beard. But… he doesn’t ride a sleigh. He fries chicken. Yes! I am talking about the KFC Grandpa.
In Japan, KFC is the King of Christmas. Today, I want to tell you about the funny differences between Christmas in the East and the West. 

The first big difference is what we eat. In the West, the star of the show is the Turkey. But let’s be honest—cooking a turkey is a nightmare. It takes five hours, the bird is massive, and the result? It usually tastes like paper! But in Asia, people don't know how to cook Turkeys. And Japan found a better way. In 1974, KFC saved the day. They basically said, 'Forget that dry, boring bird. Have some delicious, juicy fried chicken instead.' It worked perfectly. Even today, 50 years later, people in Japan would line up for long hours to get fried chicken. To put it simply: For Japanese people, Christmas is basically just the ultimate 'Crazy Thursday'!


The second big difference is about what we do. In the West, Christmas is for Family. But in East Asia—in China, Japan, Korea—Christmas is for Lovers. It is basically a second Valentine’s Day. Restaurants are full of couples. The lights are romantic. Girls get flowers. Have you heard a song of Eason Chan for Heartbroken people, with lyrics like "lonely lonely Christmas" . Even if you don't have a lover, You'll probably go out for a big meal with your friends on Christmas Eve. So, for most of Asian,  the Christmas is a day for us to have fun.

Now, you might ask: Why? Why is it so different? "It all about the New Year. In the West, Christmas is serious—it’s their time for family reunions and celebrating the new year. But here? We have our own Spring Festival for that. So for us, Christmas feels like a 'bonus.' We don't have the pressure of religious rules or heavy traditions. We can just take this imported holiday and simply enjoy the fun of it.

In the end, the world is a funny place. On one side, we have dry turkey and loud families. On the other side, we have romantic dates and a bucket of KFC fried chicken. But you know what? It doesn't matter if you eat turkey or chicken. It doesn't matter if you are with your grandma or your boyfriend. For us, Christmas is just about being happy and eating too much food. So, I hope you all have a wonderful Christmas. And remember: keep it "Finger Lickin’ Good." Thank you!
