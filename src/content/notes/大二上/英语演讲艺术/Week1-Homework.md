# Week1 Homework


## Question:
Please make a comparison of public speaking and private speaking/ conversation, and make a few bulletin points regarding their similarities and differences.


## Answer: 

Public speaking and private conversation represent two fundamentally different forms of human communication. The former is a structured, deliberate act of addressing a group, while the latter is a more spontaneous and informal exchange between individuals. 

Though distinct, they share core elements: a speaker conveying a message to an audience. In both contexts, success hinges on the speaker's ability to adapt their message to be resonant and effective for their listeners. 

Regarding their purpose, public speaking is inherently goal-oriented, designed to inform, persuade, or entertain a collective audience. Its aim is to create a connection and ultimately influence the listeners' thoughts or actions. Conversely, the purpose of a private conversation is often more fluid and relational. It can range from transactional goals, like giving instructions, to purely social ones, such as strengthening bonds by sharing personal stories with friends. 

In terms of form, the two modes diverge significantly. Public speaking is characterized by its logical structure and formal language, demanding careful preparation and rehearsal. Private conversation, on the other hand, thrives on spontaneity. It is casual in tone, unstructured in its progression, and defined by its interactive, back-and-forth dynamic.
