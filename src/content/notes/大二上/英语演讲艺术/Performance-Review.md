### 1. Strengths

*   **Language & Style**
    I used accessible vocabulary and incorporated rhetorical repetition throughout the script. This made the content easy to follow and significantly enhanced its persuasiveness.

*   **Detailed Preparation**
    I meticulously annotated the script with specific cues for pauses and hand gestures, providing a clear roadmap for the intended delivery.

*   **Topic Selection**
    The choice of "sports check-ins" was highly effective, as it is a topic closely tied to the daily lives of my classmates, ensuring their natural interest.

*   **Audience Engagement**
    By opening with relatable real-life scenarios, I successfully established a connection with the audience and created a strong sense of resonance.

### 2. Areas for Improvement

*   **Delivery & Execution**
    Since I had not fully memorized the script, I couldn't perfectly execute all the planned pauses and gestures. 
    *   **Solution:** Next time, I will conduct multiple rehearsals in front of a mirror to simulate the delivery and build muscle memory.

*   **Content & Evidence**
    The speech could be more convincing with stronger factual backing.
    *   **Solution:** In future presentations, I plan to add more concrete evidence, such as statistics or research data, to further strengthen my arguments.

### 3. Future Action Plan

1.  **Mirror Rehearsals:** Practice at least 3-5 times in front of a mirror to synchronize body language with the script.
2.  **Evidence Research:** Dedicate more time to finding supporting data during the script-writing phase.
3.  **Fluency Drills:** Aim for a higher level of memorization to ensure the delivery feels natural and confident.
