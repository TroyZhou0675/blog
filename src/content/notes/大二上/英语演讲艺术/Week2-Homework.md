# Week2 Homework


## Question:


Please make a few bulletin points in response to the following questions.

1. What are the symptoms and causes of YOUR stage fright? How can you manage it?

2. What are the ethics of public speaking?



## Answer: 


Stage fright presents with a range of physical and psychological symptoms. Physically, you might experience sweaty palms and a shaky voice. Mentally, the anxiety can cause you to forget your material, while your eyes may shift nervously, avoiding contact with the audience.

A lack of familiarity with my speech content and the intense gaze of the audience are the main triggers for my stage fright. To manage this, I focus on diligent preparation beforehand. Once on stage, I regulate my breathing to stay calm. I also try to make eye contact with the audience to get positive feedback, which helps alleviate my anxiety.

Ethical public speaking requires authenticity and data integrity. It is crucial to respect the audience by avoiding discriminatory language. Furthermore, speakers should give the audience space for independent thought and avoid imposing strong personal biases.
