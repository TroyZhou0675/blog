### 1. What makes a good speech?
When it comes to what makes a good speech, performance comes first. People may not fully understand your content but can easily moved by your performance like tone, gesture and facial expression. So we need to design the details like where our hands should be putted on. Many students may encounter the stage fright at the first times, and that makes your speech less persuasive. We can practice in front of the mirror to improve your confidence. When audience cannot tell if you're reciting or speaking, the ultimate goal is reached. And we also need to design our gestures enhance our explanation. A good speech also need a good control of facial expression. For example, closing your mouth at a small pulse during the speech makes you confident.

Second, a good speech have a good content that have strong connection with the audience. We should investigate the audience and ensure our speech is strong related to most of the audience. Then, a good speech also should avoid the moral defects like racial, gender bias. More importantly, credibility should be ensured. We should provide the figures or source of information to enhance our persuasiveness. More recommended is using figure that audience can feel intuitively, which means the figure should be tightly attached to our daily life.

Third, a good speech must have good expressiveness. We should use simple word and use repetitions to makes our speech powerful. And we should control our voice speed and avoid fast speaking. We also should make our speech more varied through say important words in high volume and make some necessary pulse.
