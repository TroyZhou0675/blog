Before the speech, I'd like to ask you to play a small game. Do not worry, it is very simple. All you need is just your hands. Now I will give you a rhythm like this (clap my hands), and your job is to repeat is exactly as you heard.

Now ,you may know that when one hand meet the other, it creates the rhythm. But for me ,it is not just the rhythm. It is also a language, the language of percussion.

My journey with percussion, however, didn't start with passion. It began with five years of painful practice, where the only goal was to pass exams and win competitions. When I started junior high, the heavy workload of my classes gave me an easy excuse to quit. 

But something shifted in high school. One day, I heard a song by the British rock band Judas Priest. The raw power and uplifting energy of rock music hit me, and suddenly, that rhythmic fire was reignited. I picked up the drumsticks again, but this time, it was only about passion, about feeling that powerful connection from my hands to my heart. 

University opened up a whole new world of rhythm. I started my own band, and last year we performed all over campus. Just last month, we were the opening act at the school's music festival for major artists like Wu Kequn. I also joined the symphony orchestra, where I was amazed by the power of symphonic percussion. We used everything from timpani to snare drums to build tension, create climactic impact, and add vibrant color. I also became fascinated by the freedom of jazz and the dynamic energy of Latin music, watching masters perform in jazz clubs like Huanglou and Goodcha.

But percussion has given me more than just music. It has brought me some of the most incredible friendships of my life. In daily life , we gather together for meals and sing along the guitar. On stage, move as one. Those moments of creating something powerful together are among my most cherished memories. 

This journey has taken me from a kid who hated to practice to someone who now uses music to connect, to understand, and to embrace the beautiful, diverse rhythms of our world. Thank you.
