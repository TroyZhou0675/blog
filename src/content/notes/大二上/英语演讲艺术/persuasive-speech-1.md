Friends, fellow students,

Picture this: 
It's the end of a long week of  midterm exams. You're exhausted. You go for your required run, but you're so tired you have to walk one lap. And just **one lap** (手势), your pace is too slow. The entire 2km you just finished? **Invalid!**

**This is** the reality of our current sports check-in system. 
停顿

The rule that requires everyone to run a kilometer in under 10 minutes might seem reasonable at first glance.
**BUT**
What about the student who is struggling with obesity and has just started their fitness journey, choosing to walk to lose weight?
What about the student with a lung or heart condition who has been advised by their doctor to avoid intense workouts?
Their **courage** to exercise within their limits should be applauded, not punished.

The rule is limited and need to be changed!

As we all know, Our bodies are not machines. Some days we feel energetic, and other days we feel tired. A system that ignores this simple fact is a **Unscientific** system. It turns exercise, something that **should** reduce stress, into another source of stress.

True fitness is about consistency and  listening to your body. Forcing a fast pace on every single day makes us want to quit.

It is time to end this limiting rule.

We need a system that values our **actions**, not just our speed. A system that understands a walk is better than **doing nothing.** 

Let's demand a system that sees us as **people**, not as **numbers** on a stopwatch.  
A system that creates more **possibilities**, not more **restrictions**.  
A system that supports our **health**, not one that **hurts** it.

Thank you.
