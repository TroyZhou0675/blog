Picture this: It's one in the morning. The only light in your room is the flashing glow from your roommate's screen. The only sound is the furious click, click, click of their mouse as they battle a digital dragon. You have an important exam at 8 a.m. You pull the covers over your head, but you can still hear it. Sound familiar? 

Or how about this: you walk in after a long day of classes, and you're met with a smell... a unique combination of three-day-old takeout and a mountain of unwashed socks. You look at the overflowing trash can and just sigh. 

These small moments, these tiny frictions, are the sparks that can ignite one of the most stressful challenges of university life. It's the topic we all whisper and complain about with our friends, but rarely tackle head-on: dormitory conflict.

Good morning, everyone. My name is Zhou Yitong, and like most of you, I've navigated this minefield. My goal today isn't to complain or to point fingers. It's to share some simple, practical strategies that can help us turn our shared space from a potential battleground into a genuine home. 

To achieve this, we will focus on three key steps. First, we'll identify the 'Big Three' silent conflict starters: mismatched expectations around cleanliness, noise, and guests. Second, I will introduce a simple, non-confrontational communication technique to help you express your needs without starting a fight. And finally, we’ll look at the ultimate secret weapon for peace: the roommate agreement."
