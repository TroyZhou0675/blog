I do not believe that an environmental tax on air travel would be an effective policy, since air travel is relatively efficient and the technology investment is hard to implement.
To elaborate, airplanes are often more efficient for long-distance transportation compared to other options such as cars and ships. For example, a single plane can carry hundreds of passengers at once, which reduces the carbon **emissions** per person. Therefore, placing an environmental tax on air travel may **do little to** reduce pollution, but will **simply**  increase travel costs for passengers .
Another reason is that an environmental tax may not efficiently reduce air travel **demand**. (描述刚需)Many people **rely on** airplanes for long-distance travel, especially for business trips or international journeys. **Even if** ticket prices increase due to additional taxes, people **may still** choose to fly because there are few practical alternatives. 
Still, some might claim that these taxes could be invested to improve relevant technology. That said, this approach may not be effective because governments may invest in technologies that take decades to develop, and it is **uncertain** which innovations will **actually** will actually reduce emissions.
