I believe the biggest mistake people make when buying technology products is being misled by advertisements and forgetting their original needs.
In many cases, people initially want to buy a device for a special purpose. However, when they browse different products online, they are often attracted by fancy features promoted in advertisements. As a result, they **gradually** forget what they actually need and **end** **up** buying products that are not very useful for them.
For example, some engineering students need a personal computer mainly to run Matlab simulations. However, they may be attracted by the **beautiful interface** and **smooth user experience** of a MacBook and decide to purchase it running the software they need.
**Although** advertisements can help people compare different products, it is **still** important for consumers to remember their original purpose when making a purchase decision.
