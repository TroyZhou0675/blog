**I don't agree** with this point since traditional university education can bring us structural knowledge system which several independent programs can not reach. 
**To be more specific**, traditional university education has courses that are deliberately and reasonably designed, thus can guide us learn knowledges step by step and finally from a comprehensive and structural knowledge system. 
**For example**, courses in computer science often include not only the engineering courses like computer formation, C programming and operating system, but also many fundamental theoretical courses like linear algebra, calculus and scattered math which are really helpful for us to fully understand upcoming courses. 
**Still**, some might claim that compared to the certification programs like computer programming, university courses are much more longer, which is a waste of time. **However**, those programs only teach how-to-use, rather than how-it-works, making it hard for us to understand those scientific theories behind skills. As a result, people receiving independent programs without university education are more likely to be replaced by AI.
