I disagree with the idea that owning many possessions necessarily(必然的)decreases the quality of life. In most cases, useful possessions can improve our daily efficiency and reduce stress, which ultimately enhances our **overall well-being.**
First, many modern products are designed to save times and energy. For example, owning a vehicle reduces commuting time, allowing people to avoid long and exhausting journeys. The time saved can be spent with family members, exercising or develop personal interests. Similarly, **household appliances** such as washing machines and dishwasher reduce physical labor, enabling individuals to focus more on meaningful activities. By improving efficiency, these possessions directly contribute to a **better work-life balance.**
**Admittedly,** some possessions requires maintenance and organization. **However,** the benefits they bring often **outweigh** these minor inconveniences. **The problems lies not in** ownership itself, **but in** excessively consumption and poor management. When possessions are chosen wisely and used responsibly, they enhance comfort, security and productivity.
Therefore, I believe that having many useful possessions, when properly managed, can significantly improve **rather than** decrease the quality of life.
